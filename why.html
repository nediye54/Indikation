Why MDG

Most problems are treated as “symptoms”. MDG treats structure: decision rights, load paths, energy limits, feedback loops.

Core idea

Structure over variables

If you change only “effort”, “motivation” or “pressure”, the same pattern returns. MDG identifies the load-bearing structure.

Decision architecture

When ownership and decision rights are unclear, friction grows, and politics replaces execution. MDG makes it explicit.

What makes it different

High leverage, low input

Few questions, designed to reduce ambiguity.
No “therapy talk”, no generic advice.
Outputs are operational: what to do, in what order, with what signals.

Stabilize vs Rebuild

Stabilize: reduce tension quickly, stop escalation.
Rebuild: design a structure that can carry future load.
MDG keeps both paths separate—so execution stays clean.

When to use MDG

If you feel “stuck”

Recurring loops, endless debates, rework, conflict, burnout signals, unclear ownership.

If growth increases fragility

More scope without new structure: complexity rises faster than capacity and decisions.

Private vs Business

Private

Personal systems: roles, routines, relationships, individual load and recovery.

Business

Organizations: decision rights, operating model, roles, cadence, KPIs, cross-team friction.
